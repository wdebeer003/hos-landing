Accounting Accuracy

Why your GP% is always wrong in other systems

This is the most common and most costly accounting error in hospitality. Hospitality OS eliminates it by design — using the same principle as a perpetual inventory ledger.

The Perpetual Inventory Principle

In a standard Xero + supplier invoice workflow, the invoice date drives when Cost of Sales hits your P&L. If your supplier invoices you on the 28th but you only sell those goods in the next month, your GP% for month 1 is understated and month 2 is overstated — every single month.

Hospitality OS uses a perpetual inventory ledger. Every transaction creates an immutable stock movement entry. Cost of Sales is only recognised at the moment of sale — never at receipt.

Step 1 — Goods received (GRV approved)
DR  Stock on Hand   (Balance Sheet)
  CR  Accounts Payable (Balance Sheet)
 
→ COS is NOT touched yet
Stock lands on the balance sheet. P&L untouched.
Step 2 — Item sold (POS order closes)
DR  Cost of Sales  (P&L — this moment)
  CR  Stock on Hand  (Balance Sheet)
 
→ COS at point of sale only
GP% is accurate to the minute, every day.

Internal Controls

Audit trail & data integrity

Every transaction is immutable once posted. Corrections happen via reversal entries — the same principle as double-entry bookkeeping.

Stock Movement Posted
Every GRV, sale, transfer, stocktake, and production batch creates an immutable stock movement entry. Cannot be edited or deleted.
Complimentary Meal Approved
Records approver ID, timestamp, reason, and recipient. Linked to Xero contra-revenue journal. Cannot be edited or deleted.
POS Session Event
Every PIN login, logout, auto-lock, manual lock, and user switch logged with timestamp and employee ID.
Void or Discount
Voids require a reason. Discounts require manager PIN approval. Both are logged and cannot be retrospectively changed.
Xero Export
Every journal posted is logged — what was exported, when, by whom. Prevents duplicate exports. Full export audit trail.
